Major Issues

Sea Level Rise

Increasing sea levels due to climate change will increase the flooding risk for many areas in New York City, especially Queens. Anti-flood technolgoies include levees and dams. Increased flooding will damage infrastructure, and could result in deaths. It is crucial that cities build infrastructure to withstand flooding.

Heat Waves

Heat waves present an increased risk to public health. Along with increasing the incidence of heat-related illnesses such as heat stroke, heat exhaustion, dehydration, and sunburns. Because the temperature is projected to rise several degrees Farenheit in the coming century, it is imperative that resources should be in place for those who might not have access to air conditioning, or may be homeless. Public cool-down spaces and shade are essential to preventing heat-related illnesses. Because of the urban island heat effect, New York City is especially vulnerable to heat waves because surfaces such as pavement and metal reflect sunlight, making the areas even hotter.
